Your wedding day timeline is the backbone of a smooth, stress-free celebration. A well-planned schedule keeps everyone on track and ensures you have time to enjoy every moment. Here's how to build yours.

## Why a Wedding Day Timeline Matters

Without a timeline:
- Vendors arrive at different times expecting different setups
- Photos get rushed or missed
- Guests wait too long between events
- You feel stressed instead of present

With a timeline:
- Every vendor knows exactly when and where to be
- You have protected time for photos without rushing
- Transitions feel seamless
- You actually enjoy your wedding day

## How to Customize Your Timeline

### Adjust for Your Ceremony Time

- **Morning wedding (10-11 AM)**: Start getting ready at 6 AM, brunch reception
- **Afternoon wedding (2-3 PM)**: Start getting ready at 8 AM, lunch/dinner reception
- **Evening wedding (5-6 PM)**: Start getting ready at 11 AM, dinner reception

### Factor in Travel Time

If your ceremony and reception are at different venues, add 30-45 minutes for travel. Account for traffic and the wedding party needing to move between locations.

### Build in Buffer Time

Add 15-30 minute buffers between major events. Things always take longer than expected, especially:

- Hair and makeup (add 15 min per person)
- Photos (always underestimated)
- Cocktail hour to dinner transition
- Moving large groups between locations

## Tips from Wedding Planners

1. **Share the timeline with everyone** - vendors, wedding party, family members, and the DJ/band
2. **Designate a timeline keeper** - someone (ideally your planner or coordinator) who keeps things moving
3. **Protect your couple time** - schedule 15 minutes alone together after the ceremony
4. **Don't over-schedule** - leave room for spontaneous moments
5. **Eat your dinner** - schedule a plate to be set aside if you're greeting guests
6. **Plan your exit** - know exactly when and how you're leaving

## Common Timeline Mistakes

- **Too many photo groupings** - limit formal family photos to 6-8 combinations
- **Not enough time for cocktail hour** - guests need at least 60 minutes
- **Speeches running long** - set a 3-5 minute limit per speech
- **Forgetting vendor meals** - schedule when vendors eat in your timeline
- **Ignoring sunset time** - check the sunset and plan golden hour photos accordingly
