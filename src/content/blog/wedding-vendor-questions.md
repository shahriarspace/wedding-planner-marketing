Choosing wedding vendors is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make during planning. The right questions can save you thousands of dollars, prevent misunderstandings, and ensure you get exactly what you're paying for.

Here are 10 questions you should ask every vendor before signing anything.

## 1. What exactly is included in the quoted price?

This is the most important question you can ask. Get a detailed breakdown of:

- What services/items are included
- What's considered an "extra" or "add-on"
- Whether setup and breakdown are included
- If travel fees apply

**Why it matters**: A $3,000 photography package that doesn't include a second shooter or digital files is very different from one that does.

## 2. What is your cancellation and refund policy?

Life happens. Understand:

- What happens if YOU need to cancel or postpone
- What happens if THEY need to cancel
- Is your deposit refundable under any circumstances?
- What's their policy for date changes?

**Why it matters**: After the pandemic, every couple should understand cancellation terms before signing.

## 3. Have you worked at our venue before?

Vendors with venue experience know:

- The best photo spots and lighting conditions
- Loading dock access and setup logistics
- The venue coordinator's preferences
- Potential challenges (noise restrictions, power availability, etc.)

**Why it matters**: Venue familiarity means fewer surprises and a smoother day.

## 4. What happens if you're sick or have an emergency on our wedding day?

Every vendor should have a backup plan:

- Do they have a substitute or associate?
- Will you meet or approve the backup?
- Is this covered in the contract?
- Will you get a refund if the backup doesn't meet expectations?

**Why it matters**: Your wedding day is non-negotiable. A professional vendor always has a Plan B.

## 5. How many events do you book per day/weekend?

Find out if they're spreading themselves thin:

- Will they be at another event the same day?
- If so, how does that affect timing?
- Are they bringing the same equipment to both events?

**Why it matters**: You want your vendor focused and fresh, not rushing from another event.

## 6. What is the payment schedule?

Understand the full financial commitment:

- How much is the deposit?
- When are remaining payments due?
- What payment methods do you accept?
- Are there fees for credit card payments?
- What happens if a payment is late?

**Why it matters**: Knowing the payment schedule helps you budget and avoid surprises.

## 7. Do you have liability insurance?

Professional vendors should carry:

- General liability insurance
- Professional liability (errors & omissions)
- Workers' compensation (if they have employees)

**Why it matters**: If a vendor causes damage to your venue or injures a guest, insurance protects everyone. Many venues require vendor insurance.

## 8. What's your communication style and response time?

Set expectations early:

- How do you prefer to communicate? (Email, phone, text)
- What's your typical response time?
- Will we have a dedicated point of contact?
- How far in advance do we do the final walkthrough?

**Why it matters**: Mismatched communication expectations are a top source of vendor frustration.

## 9. Can you provide references from recent clients?

Ask for:

- 2-3 references from weddings similar to yours (size, style, venue)
- References from the past 6-12 months (not 5 years ago)
- Permission to see full galleries or complete event coverage (not just highlight reels)

**Why it matters**: Reviews tell part of the story. Direct references tell the rest.

## 10. What do you need from us, and by when?

Turn the tables:

- What information or decisions do they need from you?
- What are their deadlines?
- What typically causes delays or issues from the couple's side?

**Why it matters**: Being a great client means your vendors do their best work.
